# Plot And Meta

This file connects the mansion narrative to the hidden experiment narrative.

## Story Spine

The player enters under pretense, navigates hospitality pressure, uncovers the house's true logic, finds a person worth rescuing, and learns that both the mansion and the interface are systems of containment.

## Recommended Act Structure

### Act 1: Invitation And Approach

- Player reaches the cavern and tests the perimeter.
- The house appears grand, bizarre, and only partly sealed.
- Early threat should be atmospheric and weird rather than directly explanatory.

### Act 2: Admission And Etiquette

- Butlers verify identity.
- The player learns the house runs on ritualized belonging.
- First successful compliance should feel like a victory that is secretly entrapment.

### Act 3: The Feast And The Trap

- Oshregaal appears.
- The player experiences his charm, menace, appetite, and refusal to let guests leave.
- The blood request and its aftermath define the emotional midpoint.

### Act 4: Back Rooms And Hidden Systems

- The player encounters Kelago, the kitchen, Plum, secret passages, and household contradictions.
- Escape becomes concrete.
- The mansion stops feeling like a single scene and starts feeling like a machine.

### Act 5: Escape, Betrayal, Or Containment

- The player commits to a route.
- One or more objectives resolve: rescue, theft, sabotage, assassination, exposure.
- The meta-story recontextualizes what success means.

## Primary Mission Baseline

Use this as the main authored route unless the project intentionally pivots.

- Rescue Plum.
- Escape the mansion alive.
- Discover enough of the experiment to understand that survival and freedom are different goals.

## Optional Branches Worth Supporting

- Steal the Grey Grin Blade and leave.
- Destroy or poison the black wind tree.
- Expose black wind trade evidence.
- Cut a bargain with Nathema.
- Attempt to kill Oshregaal.

## Endings Framework

### Minimum good ending

- The player escapes the mansion.
- Plum survives.
- The player is no longer obedient to the visible fiction.

### Strong better ending

- The player escapes with Plum and meaningful evidence, knowledge, or leverage.
- They understand the experiment is still active.

### Dark bargain endings

- Escape by empowering Nathema.
- Escape by accepting mutation or service.
- Escape physically while remaining trapped by the experiment's broader shell.

### Failure endings

- Social capture.
- Sealed-room containment.
- Absorption into Oshregaal's routines.
- Meta-layer reinterpretation of failure as successful conditioning.

## Meta-Story Baseline

The player is an experiment subject inside a parser-like shell.

There are at least three hidden forces:

- operators running the scenario and measuring responses;
- a disruptive outside or inside actor able to leak messages;
- the shell itself, which may be older or less stable than its current operators admit.

## Meta Delivery Stages

### Stage 1: Ambiguous Interference

- short side-channel messages;
- formatting anomalies;
- eerie but deniable lines that could be read as style.

### Stage 2: Instrumental Assistance

- upgrades arrive in ways that feel useful and suspicious;
- the system appears to optimize the player's experience while narrowing it;
- the map and inventory panels are not present at the beginning and should arrive as hacker-delivered hacks rather than neutral defaults;
- leaked messages reference baselines, compliance, tolerance, or attachment.

### Stage 3: Explicit Entrapment

- the player understands the parser itself is part of the prison;
- escape from Oshregaal may not equal escape from the experiment;
- outside messages become contradictory and strategic.

## Rules For Merging Meta With Mansion Fiction

- Meta messages should not interrupt the most important first impressions of a room.
- Early meta text should sound procedural, not melodramatic.
- The hacker voice should sound urgent, terse, and materially useful.
- When the hacker grants a panel, the UI should treat that as a dramatic beat, not a silent quality-of-life toggle.
- Oshregaal should remain more emotionally vivid than any off-screen operator.
- Meta escalation should follow story milestones, not only turn counts.

## Recommended Milestone Hooks

- first admission to the house;
- first direct interaction with Oshregaal;
- first evidence of a hidden exit;
- first visit to a forbidden room;
- first contact with Plum;
- first irreversible act of betrayal or sabotage;
- first escape attempt;
- discovery of the circle, folded space, or black wind source.

## Recommended UI Upgrade Fiction

These upgrades should feel like contested interventions inside the shell rather than benign UI growth.

- inventory panel: granted later by the hacker as an illicit retention hack;
- status readout: framed as subject monitoring;
- map panel or map fragments: granted later by the hacker as navigation-debugging contraband;
- memory panel: a later unstable display of pseudo-memory addresses that the player can eventually learn to probe and hack by interacting with the game's different systems;
- transcript search or memory recall: framed as cognitive support or shell leakage depending on source;
- hint surfacing: framed as correction, not kindness.

## Meta Questions To Keep Open

- Are the operators fully in control of the shell?
- Is the hacker a former subject, current captive, or parasitic process?
- Is the Oshregaal fiction imported, simulated, remembered, or partially real?
- Is the player's role unique, or one in a line of prior runs?

Do not answer all of these early.
